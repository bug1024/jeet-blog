# Visual directions

## Selection table

| Direction | Prefer for | Core visual language | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A. Restrained editorial metaphor | systems, organization, judgment, choices, abstract work lessons | minimal editorial illustration, tactile paper or dry brush, quiet wit, one human-scale metaphor | literal diagrams, crowded symbolism, cute cartooning |
| B. Quiet technical photography | AI Coding, engineering practice, architecture, late-night making | believable workspace or tool detail, practical light, muted color, evidence of use, human presence by trace | cyberpunk, RGB lighting, perfect showroom desk, readable fake code |
| C. Documentary still-life / spatial narrative | family, time, place, memory, personal change, mixed work-life essays | ordinary objects or spaces arranged by lived meaning, natural light, restrained grain, specific atmosphere | generic lifestyle stock, sentimental props, artificial nostalgia |

Choose one direction. Do not hybridize all three in one cover.

## A. Restrained editorial metaphor

Use as the default when the article discusses a mechanism, tension, responsibility, or choice that cannot be honestly photographed.

Good metaphor construction:

- Keep one actor, one system, and one action.
- Translate the article's mechanism, not its nouns.
- Show scale or dependency through composition.
- Let the unusual relationship carry meaning without explanatory labels.

Example for “系统在跑，其实是人在扛”:

```text
A restrained editorial illustration on warm mist-white paper: one ordinary maintenance worker quietly braces a slightly misaligned structural beam that supports an otherwise precise mechanical system. The machinery continues beyond the frame, but only a few large geometric components are visible. Ink-black linework and muted graphite textures, with one small steel-blue repair plate as the only color accent. Generous but purposeful breathing room, human scale against system scale, quiet and unsentimental. No text, no labels, no servers, no gears filling the frame.
```

Do not default to a giant gear. Search for a less literal support relationship: a shim under a table, a hand keeping two tracks aligned, a temporary brace inside an immaculate structure, or one repaired joint carrying a larger load.

## B. Quiet technical photography

Use when physical making, coding practice, tools, or a real working moment is central to the article.

Good scene construction:

- Prefer a fragment of a real workspace over a full desk tour.
- Use a plausible screen glow, task lamp, worn keyboard edge, notebook correction, cable, or half-finished object.
- Keep screens oblique or out of focus so no fake text becomes content.
- Show work in progress, not technology as spectacle.

Example for an AI Coding essay:

```text
Natural editorial photography of a lived-in engineer's desk at two in the morning, framed around a hand moving between a worn keyboard and a paper notebook with non-readable marks. A monitor sits partly out of frame with soft neutral light, no legible interface. Ink-dark room, mist-white paper, one muted steel-blue object near the working area. Practical lamp light, subdued contrast, realistic imperfections, quiet renewed concentration. No neon, no RGB, no robot, no hologram, no cinematic blue wash, no text or watermark.
```

## C. Documentary still-life / spatial narrative

Use when the article's meaning sits in lived objects, place, family, memory, or the passage between life stages.

Good scene construction:

- Anchor the frame in one specific trace: a child's drawing beside a work notebook, a packed box near a window, a repaired household object, keys from different cities, or shadows changing across a familiar surface.
- Let the relationship between objects imply time or choice.
- Keep emotion observational rather than sentimental.
- Prefer daylight, overcast light, or one practical household source.

Example for work and family:

```text
Quiet documentary still-life on a real dining table after the household has gone to sleep: a closed work notebook, one child's small handmade paper object, and a cup leaving a faint ring, arranged naturally rather than styled. Soft side light from an ordinary lamp, muted warm neutrals with ink-dark shadows; a tiny steel-blue clip is the only cool accent. Specific, lived-in, restrained, no posed people, no nostalgia filter, no text, no watermark.
```

## Rejection test

Reject or regenerate when any answer is “yes”:

1. Would the image fit an unrelated technology article equally well?
2. Is the main idea carried by text inside the image?
3. Does it look like a corporate campaign, public-account thumbnail, or AI prompt showcase?
4. Is technology represented by a robot, glowing brain, circuit, code rain, or neon interface?
5. Is steel blue used as atmosphere rather than a precise accent?
6. Are there several competing metaphors or anomalies?
7. Does the image lose its focal relationship at small size?
8. Is a real author photograph available that tells the truth better?
